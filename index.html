Who LeanOps is for

LeanOps is built for small and mid-size manufacturers who need real visibility but don’t have the budget or time for a full MES implementation.

  • High-mix, low-volume operations
  • Manual or semi-manual assembly lines and cells
  • Shops relying on whiteboards, spreadsheets, or tribal knowledge
  • Supervisors who spend hours walking the floor to get updates

Why LeanOps exists

Most MES and OEE systems are built for machine-heavy, fully automated plants with big IT teams. But many factories don’t need machine connectivity or a giant platform — they just need a clear, real-time picture of what’s running, what’s next, and where flow is stuck.

LeanOps focuses on the essentials of running a production department: jobs, stations, materials, and pacing. It’s the digital version of how strong supervisors already run the floor — just more visible, more consistent, and easier to manage every day.

How LeanOps compares

vs. heavy MES platforms

Many MES tools (and no-code platforms) are built for large, automated plants. They require long implementations, developers, and big budgets — and ship with hundreds of features your team will never use.

LeanOps is intentionally lightweight: deploy a pilot in a single department, get value in days, and expand only when it earns its place on the floor.

vs. machine-focused OEE systems

OEE tools focus on machine uptime and spindle utilization. LeanOps focuses on something different: people, jobs, and flow. It’s ideal for manual or semi-manual assembly, mixed-model operations, and departments where the constraint is coordination, not machine runtime.

The real alternative to LeanOps isn’t another app — it’s whiteboards, spreadsheets, and supervisors chasing updates all day.
